Hiya!

I'm a front-end web developer. Here are a few more things about me:

  • I've been the front-end dev at Gigmor since summer 2016, where I use ES6 JavaScript, React/Redux, Sass, Foundation, NPM, GIT, Puppeteer, XD, and Photoshop.
  • Gigmor hired me right out of a full-stack developer bootcamp at General Assembly, where I learned Ruby/Ruby on Rails, JQuery, Bootstrap, and the MEAN stack.
  • Before becoming a full-time developer, I was a writer, editor, and college instructor who made websites for fun and shameless self-promotion.
  • I have degrees from U.C. Berkeley and USC in Anthropology and writing, respectively.
  • My commute is long; podcasts are my friends.

Projects

Gigmor

A jobs marketplace connecting musicians and venues, built from the ground up in React by three- to five-person team.

Kaitlin.Today

A hand-coded minimalist blog with a custom flexbox grid created with the narrow purpose of delivering a digestible quantity of news to one person — the titular Kaitlin — every day.

Near Hear

A Node.js-based app built in a three-person team with the Google Maps and Bands in Town APIs, allowing users to find what concerts are happening near them and get ticket information.

Pokébook

A full-CRUD, Bootsrapped, Ruby on Rails app created during a one-week sprint that allows users to make an account and maintain a list of their favorite Pokémon.

Emojination

A MEAN-stack app that allows users to write stories based on emoji prompts. Featured Materialize styling that cycled through color schemes with a click and content that was served up with some randomization.

Bright Lights, Big Kitty

A logic-driven card game build with JavaScript, JQuery HTML, CSS, and a gratuitous quantity of cat gifs.
